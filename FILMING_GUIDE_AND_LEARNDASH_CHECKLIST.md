# Freemium Course: Production Guide

This document covers two things: how to film your lesson videos on your phone or tablet, and how to set up the course in LearnDash.

---

## Part 1: Video Filming Guide (Mobile & Tablet)

Filming on your phone or tablet is not a compromise — it is the message. It proves the entire premise of this course: that a powerful device in your hand is all you need.

### **Your Setup Checklist (Before You Film)**

| Item | What to Do |
|---|---|
| **Location** | Choose a quiet room with a plain or tidy background. A bookshelf, a clean wall, or a window behind you all work well. |
| **Lighting** | Face a window or a lamp. Light should hit your face, not come from behind you. Natural daylight is ideal. |
| **Sound** | Use a pair of earbuds with a built-in microphone (the ones that came with your phone work perfectly). This dramatically improves audio quality. |
| **Stability** | Prop your phone against a stack of books, or use a simple phone tripod (available for under $15 online). Eye-level is best. |
| **Framing** | Film in landscape (horizontal) mode. Your face and shoulders should fill the top two-thirds of the frame. |
| **Clothing** | Wear a solid colour. Avoid busy patterns, stripes, or white. Navy, burgundy, and dark green all look excellent on camera. |
| **Battery** | Charge your device to at least 80% before filming. |
| **Storage** | Make sure you have at least 2GB of free storage space. |

---

### **Your Filming Process (For Each Lesson)**

Follow this sequence for every video:

1.  **Read the script out loud twice** before you start recording. You are not memorising it; you are becoming familiar with the flow.
2.  **Set up your shot** using the checklist above.
3.  **Record a 10-second test clip** and play it back. Check the sound, the framing, and the lighting before you film the full video.
4.  **Film the full video.** If you stumble, pause for two seconds and continue from the last sentence. Do not stop and restart — pauses are easy to cut in editing.
5.  **Film a second take** of any section that felt off. You can choose the best one in editing.
6.  **Review your clip** before moving on.

## Part 2: LearnDash Setup Checklist

Follow these steps in order inside your WordPress dashboard.

### **Step 1: Create the Course**

- Go to **LearnDash LMS → Courses → Add New**
- Title: `The New Frontier: A 50+ Guide to Mobile & Tablet Mastery`
- Paste the **Course Welcome copy** from `LEARNDASH_PRODUCTION_COPY.md`
- Set **Access Mode** to `Open`
- Upload a **Featured Image** (a strong photo of Brian with a phone or tablet)
- Click **Publish**

---

### **Step 2: Create the 3 Sections (Modules)**

- Inside the course, go to the **Builder** tab
- Add **Section:** `Module 1: Your Device is a World-Changer`
- Add **Section:** `Module 2: From Consumer to Creator`
- Add **Section:** `Module 3: Build Your Global Community`

---

### **Step 3: Create the 6 Lessons**

For each lesson:
- Go to **LearnDash LMS → Lessons → Add New**
- Paste the lesson title and body copy from `LEARNDASH_PRODUCTION_COPY.md`
- Add the **video embed** at the top of the lesson (YouTube or Vimeo link)
- Assign the lesson to the correct **Course and Section**
- Click **Publish**

| Lesson | Section |
|---|---|
| The Portal in Your Pocket | Module 1 |
| Your First 15-Minute Mobile Practice | Module 1 |
| Tell Your Story with Mobile Video | Module 2 |
| Design Graphics that Stop the Scroll | Module 2 |
| Find Your People on Mobile | Module 3 |
| Join the 50+ TechBridge Movement | Module 3 |

---

### **Step 4: Create the 3 Quizzes**

For each quiz:
- Go to **LearnDash LMS → Quizzes → Add New**
- Title the quiz (e.g., `Module 1 Check-in`)
- Add the questions from `LEARNDASH_PRODUCTION_COPY.md`
- Set **Passing Score** to 50%
- Assign the quiz to the correct **Course and Section**
- Click **Publish**

---

### **Step 5: Upload Your Videos**

- Upload each finished video to **YouTube (Unlisted)** or **Vimeo**
- Copy the embed link and paste it into the top of the corresponding lesson in LearnDash
- Do not host videos directly on WordPress — it will slow your site down significantly

---

### **Step 6: Test the Course**

- Log out of WordPress
- Visit the course page as a guest
- Click through every lesson and quiz to confirm everything works
- Check that the final CTA button (Upgrade to Full Course) is visible and links correctly

---

### **Step 7: Add the Course Link to Your Nav Bar**

- Go to **Appearance → Menus**
- Add the course page link to your main navigation
- Suggested label: `Free Course` or `Start Here`

---

**You are ready to launch. The content is written. The scripts are ready. The setup is mapped. All that remains is to press record.**
